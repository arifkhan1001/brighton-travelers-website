About Brighton

A vibrant seaside city where history meets bohemian spirit

The Jewel of the South Coast

Nestled on the sparkling south coast of England, Brighton has been enchanting visitors since the 18th century when the Prince Regent first made it his royal playground. Today it's one of the UK's most vibrant and inclusive cities.

From the iconic pebble beach and stunning Regency architecture to its thriving arts scene, quirky independent shops and legendary nightlife, Brighton blends seaside charm with cosmopolitan flair like nowhere else.

Often called "London-by-the-Sea", this buzzing city is home to colourful street art, world-class festivals, a famous foodie culture, and a spirit of creativity that's hard to resist.

Must-Visit Places

Brighton's iconic landmarks you simply can't miss

Iconic

Brighton Palace Pier

The city's most famous landmark — a Victorian pleasure pier with fairground rides, arcades, and stunning sea views stretching to the horizon.

Heritage

Royal Pavilion

An extraordinary Indo-Saracenic palace built for King George IV. Its opulent interior and exotic gardens make it a true architectural wonder.

Classic

Brighton Beach

Famous pebble beaches with colourful beach huts, lively promenade, and spectacular sunsets that paint the sky in shades of orange and pink.

Shopping

The Lanes

A maze of narrow, winding streets filled with antique shops, boutiques, jewellers, and cosy cafés — a treasure hunter's paradise.

Modern

British Airways i360

The world's slimmest observation tower rises 162 metres, offering breathtaking 360-degree panoramic views of the coast and South Downs.

Food & Drink

Savour Brighton's legendary culinary scene

Must Try

Fish & Chips

No visit to Brighton is complete without freshly battered fish and crispy chips enjoyed on the seafront. Try Bankers, The Regency, or Bardsley's for the best!

Chill Vibes

Seafront Cafés

Sip artisan coffee while watching the waves. Brighton's beachfront is lined with charming cafés serving everything from pastries to brunch plates.

Foodie

Street Food Markets

From the bustling Open Market to pop-up stalls, Brighton's street food scene offers flavours from around the world — tacos, bao buns, curries and more.

Upcoming Events

Mark your calendar for Brighton's best experiences

May 2026

Brighton Festival

England's largest mixed-arts festival returns with theatre, music, dance, circus, art, film and literature events across the city.

June 2026

Brighton Pride

One of the UK's biggest and most colourful Pride celebrations — featuring a parade, live music, community events and a legendary party atmosphere.

July 2026

Paddle Round the Pier

Europe's biggest free beach and watersports festival with paddleboarding, kayaking, surfing, and beach volleyball right on Brighton seafront.

August 2026

Brighton Food Festival

A three-week celebration of Sussex food and drink featuring tastings, cookery demos, supper clubs, and pop-up dining experiences.

November 2026

Burning the Clocks

Brighton's magical winter solstice lantern parade — handmade paper and willow lanterns light up the streets before a spectacular bonfire on the beach.

Nearby Escapes

Beautiful day trips just minutes from Brighton

Nature

Seven Sisters Cliffs

A series of dramatic chalk cliffs on the South Downs — one of the most photographed coastlines in the world. Perfect for coastal walks.

Historic

Lewes

A charming medieval county town with a Norman castle, independent bookshops, the famous Guy Fawkes Night celebrations, and rolling South Downs views.

Scenic

Devil's Dyke

A spectacular V-shaped valley carved into the South Downs with panoramic views stretching across the Weald. A favourite for hikers and paragliders.

Fun Facts

Surprising tidbits about our favourite seaside city

🐠

Oldest Aquarium

Brighton is home to Sea Life Brighton, the world's oldest operating aquarium — open since 1872!

🏙️

London-by-the-Sea

Brighton is nicknamed "London-by-the-Sea" because it's just 60 minutes from the capital by train.

🌈

UK's LGBTQ+ Capital

Brighton is widely recognised as the LGBTQ+ capital of the UK, famous for its inclusive and welcoming spirit.

🎡

Electric Railway First

Volk's Electric Railway, built in 1883, is the oldest operating electric railway in the world — and it runs along Brighton seafront!
