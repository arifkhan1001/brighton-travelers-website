Best Food Spots in Brighton

Brighton is a foodie's paradise. From fresh fish & chips by the sea to world-class vegan cuisine — here's where to eat in Brighton UK.

← Back to Blog

Brighton's food scene is one of the most vibrant and diverse in the UK. With more vegetarian restaurants per capita than anywhere else in Britain, a thriving street food culture, award-winning seafood restaurants and cosy independent cafés, the best food spots in Brighton cater to every palate and budget.

Classic Brighton Eats – Fish & Chips

No visit to Brighton is complete without proper fish and chips by the beach. There's something magical about eating battered cod and chunky chips with the sea breeze and the sound of waves. Here are the top spots for Brighton's most iconic dish:

🐟
Classic British

Brighton Food Factory

Traditional fish and chips done right – fresh battered cod with proper chunky chips. A Brighton institution sitting right on the seafront for that authentic British seaside experience.

📍 Kings Road, Brighton Seafront

Best Fine Dining in Brighton

Brighton punches well above its weight when it comes to fine dining. These award-winning restaurants are among the best food spots in Brighton for a special occasion:

🌊
Fine Dining

The Salt Room

Modern British seafood restaurant with panoramic sea views and locally sourced Sussex produce. One of Brighton's most celebrated dining experiences — the freshest fish, served beautifully.

📍 Brighton Seafront

🍝
Italian

Cin Cin

A tiny, buzzing pasta bar turning out some of the best handmade pasta outside Italy. The menu changes daily based on fresh, seasonal ingredients. Book ahead — it fills up fast.

📍 Vine Street, Brighton

Best Vegan & Vegetarian Restaurants in Brighton

Brighton is the UK's vegan capital. With more plant-based restaurants per person than anywhere else in Britain, you won't struggle to find incredible meat-free dining:

🥦
Vegetarian

Terre à Terre

One of the UK's most celebrated vegetarian restaurants, serving inventive plant-based dishes since 1993. A Brighton institution that proves vegetarian food can be exciting, creative and truly delicious.

📍 East Street, Brighton

Best Brunch Spots in Brighton

Brighton is made for long, lazy brunches. Whether you're after eggs benedict overlooking the sea, vegan pancakes in North Laine, or a full English in a retro diner, the Brighton brunch scene is exceptional:

☕
Brunch

Presuming Ed's

Brighton's favourite brunch spot — laidback vibes, strong coffee and legendary pancake stacks. The queue out the door on weekends tells you everything you need to know.

📍 Sydney Street, North Laine

🏖️
Beachside Café

Lucky Beach Café

A beachside gem with your toes practically in the pebbles. Great burgers, fresh cocktails, and the best sunsets in Brighton. Casual, relaxed, and utterly charming.

📍 Brighton Seafront

Street Food & Markets in Brighton

Brighton's street food culture is thriving. The Brighton street food scene includes vibrant weekend markets, pop-up food stalls and cult favourite takeaways:

🍔
Street Food

Burger Brothers

Cult-favourite smash burgers that have locals queueing out the door every weekend. The double smash with special sauce is a Brighton legend — simple, honest, incredible.

📍 North Laine, Brighton

🍛
South Indian

Curry Leaf Café

Award-winning South Indian and Sri Lankan cuisine in a cosy, vibrant setting. Crispy dosas, fragrant curries and spectacular thalis that transport you straight to Chennai.

📍 Ship Street, Brighton

💡 Pro Tip: The Brighton Food Festival takes place every August — a three-week celebration of Sussex food and drink with tastings, cookery demos, supper clubs and pop-up dining experiences. Don't miss it!
